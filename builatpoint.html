ENGLISH SYNTAX STRUCTURE

1. BASIC SENTENCE STRUCTURES

S + V → She sleeps.
S + V + O → She reads books.
S + V + C → She is happy.
S + V + O + O → She gave me a gift.
S + V + O + C → They made him captain.
S + V + A → She works in Delhi.

2. PARTS OF SPEECH
Noun → dog, city, happiness
Pronoun → he, they, it
Verb → run, eat, think
Adjective → big, happy
Adverb → quickly, very
Preposition → in, on, at
Conjunction → and, but, because
Interjection → oh!, wow!

3. PHRASE STRUCTURES
Noun Phrase → the big house
Verb Phrase → is running fast
Adjective Phrase → very happy
Adverb Phrase → quite slowly
Prepositional Phrase → in the room

4. CLAUSE STRUCTURES
Independent Clause → I am learning English.
Noun Clause → I know that he is here.
Adjective Clause → The boy who is running.
Adverb Clause → I came because you called.

5. SENTENCE TYPES
Simple → I eat.
Compound → I eat and she drinks.
Complex → I eat because I am hungry.
Compound-Complex → I eat and she drinks because we are tired.

6. SENTENCE PURPOSE
Declarative → You are here.
Interrogative → Are you here?
Imperative → Sit down.
Exclamatory → What a day!

7. TENSE & ASPECT
Present → I eat
Past → I ate
Future → I will eat

Simple → I eat
Continuous → I am eating
Perfect → I have eaten
Perfect Continuous → I have been eating

8. VOICE
Active → She wrote a letter.
Passive → A letter was written by her.

9. MODAL VERBS
can, could
may, might
must
shall, should
will, would

10. WORD ORDER
Subject + Verb + Object
Example → She eats apples.

11. MODIFIERS & AGREEMENT
He runs (correct)
He run (incorrect)
Adjectives modify nouns
Adverbs modify verbs/adjectives

12. QUESTIONS & NEGATION
Do you like it?
Where are you going?
I do not like it.
She is not coming.

13. CONDITIONALS
Zero → If you heat ice, it melts.
First → If it rains, I will stay.
Second → If I were rich, I would travel.
Third → If I had studied, I would have passed.

14. COMPLEX STRUCTURES
Reported Speech → He said that he was tired.
Relative Clause → The man who came yesterday
Cleft Sentence → It is you who broke it.
Inversion → Never have I seen this.
================ PARTS OF SPEECH (शब्द भेद) FULL GUIDE =================

1. NOUN (संज्ञा)
Definition → A noun is the name of a person, place, thing or idea
(संज्ञा वह शब्द है जो किसी व्यक्ति, स्थान, वस्तु या भाव का नाम बताता है)

Syntax Structure:
Noun = Subject / Object

Examples:
Ram is a boy (राम एक लड़का है)
Delhi is a city (दिल्ली एक शहर है)
Honesty is good (ईमानदारी अच्छी है)

Types:
Proper Noun → Ram, India (व्यक्तिवाचक संज्ञा)
Common Noun → boy, city (जातिवाचक संज्ञा)
Abstract Noun → love, happiness (भाववाचक संज्ञा)
Collective Noun → team, army (समूहवाचक संज्ञा)
Material Noun → gold, water (पदार्थवाचक संज्ञा)

2. PRONOUN (सर्वनाम)
Definition → A pronoun is used in place of a noun
(सर्वनाम संज्ञा के स्थान पर प्रयोग होता है)

Syntax Structure:
Pronoun + Verb / Object

Examples:
He is running (वह दौड़ रहा है)
She is happy (वह खुश है)
They are playing (वे खेल रहे हैं)

Types:
Personal → I, you, he (व्यक्तिवाचक)
Possessive → my, your (स्वामित्व)
Reflexive → myself, yourself (आत्मवाचक)
Demonstrative → this, that (संकेतवाचक)
Relative → who, which (संबंधवाचक)

3. VERB (क्रिया)
Definition → A verb shows action or state
(क्रिया कार्य या स्थिति बताती है)

Syntax Structure:
Subject + Verb + Object

Examples:
Ram eats mango (राम आम खाता है)
She runs fast (वह तेज दौड़ती है)
They are sleeping (वे सो रहे हैं)

Types:
Main Verb → eat, run (मुख्य क्रिया)
Helping Verb → is, am, are (सहायक क्रिया)
Modal Verb → can, may (मोडल क्रिया)

4. ADJECTIVE (विशेषण)
Definition → A word that describes a noun
(जो संज्ञा की विशेषता बताए)

Syntax Structure:
Adjective + Noun

Examples:
Big house (बड़ा घर)
Beautiful girl (सुंदर लड़की)
Happy boy (खुश लड़का)

5. ADVERB (क्रिया विशेषण)
Definition → A word that describes a verb, adjective or adverb
(जो क्रिया की विशेषता बताए)

Syntax Structure:
Verb + Adverb

Examples:
He runs fast (वह तेज दौड़ता है)
She speaks loudly (वह जोर से बोलती है)

Types:
Manner → quickly (तरीका)
Time → now (समय)
Place → here (स्थान)
Degree → very (मात्रा)

6. PREPOSITION (सम्बन्धबोधक)
Definition → Shows relation between words
(दो शब्दों के बीच संबंध बताता है)

Syntax Structure:
Preposition + Noun/Pronoun

Examples:
in the room (कमरे में)
on the table (टेबल पर)
with him (उसके साथ)

7. CONJUNCTION (समुच्चयबोधक)
Definition → Joins words or sentences
(दो शब्द या वाक्य जोड़ता है)

Syntax Structure:
Sentence + Conjunction + Sentence

Examples:
I eat and I sleep (मैं खाता हूँ और सोता हूँ)
He is rich but unhappy (वह अमीर है लेकिन दुखी है)

Types:
and, but, or, because (और, लेकिन, या, क्योंकि)

8. INTERJECTION (विस्मयादिबोधक)
Definition → Expresses sudden emotion
(अचानक भावना व्यक्त करता है)

Syntax Structure:
Interjection + !

Examples:
Wow! (वाह!)
Oh! (ओह!)
Alas! (हाय!)

================ END OF PARTS OF SPEECH =================

================ MODAL VERBS (सहायक क्रियाएँ) FULL STRUCTURE =================

MODAL VERBS are helping verbs that show ability, permission, possibility, advice, obligation, etc.
(Modal verbs वे सहायक क्रियाएँ हैं जो क्षमता, अनुमति, संभावना, सलाह और बाध्यता दिखाती हैं)

================ BASIC MODAL STRUCTURE =================
Subject + Modal + V1 + Object

Example:
I can swim (मैं तैर सकता हूँ)
She can sing (वह गा सकती है)

================ 1. CAN (सकना - क्षमता/अनुमति) =================
Structure → Subject + can + V1

Use:
Ability (योग्यता)
Permission (अनुमति)

Examples:
I can play cricket (मैं क्रिकेट खेल सकता हूँ)
She can speak English (वह अंग्रेजी बोल सकती है)
Can I go? (क्या मैं जा सकता हूँ?)

Negative:
I cannot / can’t + V1
I cannot swim (मैं तैर नहीं सकता)

Question:
Can + Subject + V1?
Can you help me? (क्या तुम मेरी मदद कर सकते हो?)

================ 2. COULD (सकता था / विनम्रता) =================
Structure → Subject + could + V1

Use:
Past ability (भूतकाल क्षमता)
Polite request (विनम्र अनुरोध)

Examples:
I could run fast (मैं तेज दौड़ सकता था)
Could you help me? (क्या आप मदद कर सकते हैं?)

================ 3. MAY (संभावना / अनुमति) =================
Structure → Subject + may + V1

Use:
Possibility (संभावना)
Permission (अनुमति)

Examples:
It may rain (बारिश हो सकती है)
May I come in? (क्या मैं अंदर आ सकता हूँ?)

Negative:
may not → may not come (नहीं आ सकता)

================ 4. MIGHT (कम संभावना) =================
Structure → Subject + might + V1

Examples:
It might rain (शायद बारिश हो)
He might come (वह शायद आए)

================ 5. MUST (जरूरी / बाध्यता) =================
Structure → Subject + must + V1

Use:
Strong obligation (अनिवार्यता)
Strong belief (पक्का विश्वास)

Examples:
You must study (तुम्हें पढ़ना चाहिए)
You must obey rules (तुम्हें नियम मानने चाहिए)

Negative:
must not → You must not smoke (तुम्हें धूम्रपान नहीं करना चाहिए)

================ 6. SHOULD (सलाह / सुझाव) =================
Structure → Subject + should + V1

Examples:
You should work hard (तुम्हें मेहनत करनी चाहिए)
She should eat healthy food (उसे अच्छा खाना खाना चाहिए)

Negative:
should not → You should not lie (तुम्हें झूठ नहीं बोलना चाहिए)

================ 7. WILL (भविष्य) =================
Structure → Subject + will + V1

Use:
Future action (भविष्य कार्य)

Examples:
I will go (मैं जाऊँगा)
She will come tomorrow (वह कल आएगी)

Negative:
will not → I will not go (मैं नहीं जाऊँगा)

Question:
Will + Subject + V1?
Will you come? (क्या तुम आओगे?)

================ 8. WOULD (विनम्रता / कल्पना) =================
Structure → Subject + would + V1

Examples:
I would like tea (मैं चाय लेना चाहूँगा)
Would you help me? (क्या आप मेरी मदद करेंगे?)

Use:
Polite request (विनम्र अनुरोध)
Imaginary situation (काल्पनिक स्थिति)

================ MODAL PERFECT STRUCTURES =================
1. Must have + V3 → पक्का अनुमान
He must have gone (वह जरूर गया होगा)

2. Should have + V3 → पछतावा
You should have studied (तुम्हें पढ़ना चाहिए था)

3. Could have + V3 → संभावना थी पर नहीं हुआ
He could have won (वह जीत सकता था)

4. Might have + V3 → शायद हुआ होगा
He might have come (वह शायद आया होगा)

================ QUICK RULE =================
Modal + V1 ALWAYS
✔ I can go
✔ She should study
❌ I can goes (wrong)

================ END OF MODAL VERBS =================

ADVERB STRUCTURES

PREPOSITION STRUCTURES
Preposition + Noun → in room (कमरे में)

PHRASE STRUCTURES

Noun Clause → that he came (कि वह आया)
Adjective Clause → who is running (जो दौड़ रहा है)
Adverb Clause → when he came (जब वह आया)

TENSE STRUCTURES
Present Simple → Subject + V1 → I eat (मैं खाता हूँ)

Past Perfect → Subject + had + V3 → I had eaten
